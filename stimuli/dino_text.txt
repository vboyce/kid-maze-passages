Tyrannosaurus rex is one of the most famous dinosaurs ever. 
[trex_recon.jpg| by Nobu Tamura (CC-BY-SA)] 
For such a huge, powerful hunter, it had surprisingly tiny arms.
T-rex was about 40 feet long and could weigh as much as 9 tons. 
Its arms were only about 3 feet long. 
[trex_forelimb.jpeg | By Eduard Solà (CC-BY)]
With arms that short, it couldn't even reach its own mouth. 
Why would such a fierce predator have arms that seem almost useless?
[trex_skeleton_2.jpg| by AbstractionBlue (CC-BY)]
Scientists have known for decades that T-rex isn't the only dinosaur like this. 
Fossils from several other dinosaur families show the same pattern. 
[Carnotaurus_skeleton.jpg | by https://www.flickr.com/photos/23992608@N06/ (CC-BY)]
They too had tiny arms, giant bodies, and huge heads with powerful jaws. 
These groups were only distantly related, yet these same features evolved independently multiple times. 
What could have driven dinosaurs to separately develop small arms so many times?
[Carnotaurus.png | by Fred Wierum (CC-BY-SA)]
To understand why, scientists measured skull sizes alongside arm sizes across many different species. 
[fossil.jpg |]
A clear pattern emerged. 
Where skulls grew bigger and more robust, the arms tended to get shorter and the overall body size increased. 
[trex_skull.jpg|]
One idea is that as the prey animals got bigger and tougher, a powerful bite became more and more important. 
[Stegosaurus_TD.png | by TotalDino (CC-BY)]
Over millions of years, skulls grew larger and jaws grew more powerful. 
As the skull became the main tool for catching prey, the arms were no longer needed. 
[trex_head.jpg|]
Growing large arms takes energy, so gradually the arms got smaller and smaller.
Not all dinosaurs followed this path. 
Plant-eating dinosaurs kept their smaller heads and longer arms. 
[herbivore.png|]
So did dinosaurs that hunted in different ways. 
[Compsognathus_BW.jpeg | by Nobu Tamura (CC-BY-SA)]
T-rex's tiny arms may look strange, but they weren't a flaw.
By that point, T-rex's jaws had simply taken over the job. 
[trex_skull.jpg|]
The tiny arms were a consequence of the skull becoming T-rex's main weapon. 
It was becoming one of the most powerful predators on Earth.
[trex_recon.jpg| by Nobu Tamura (CC-BY-SA)]
